## 1. Executive Summary

Meridian Hospital, a 600-bed public healthcare provider, is facing significant operational inefficiencies and patient safety risks due to reliance on paper-based patient records.

- **£4.2M annual financial loss**
- **18% increase in medication errors (YoY)**
- **High regulatory exposure (CQC compliance risk)**

A previous EHR pilot (2016) failed due to poor implementation, not technology limitations.

### ✅ Proposed Solution
Full-scale **Electronic Health Records (EHR) implementation** supported by:
- Structured change management  
- Clinical engagement  
- Role-based training  
- Workflow-aligned system configuration  

### 📈 Expected Outcomes
- Break-even within **3–4 years**  
- Elimination of inefficiencies  
- Improved patient safety  
- Regulatory compliance  

---

## 2. Problem Statement

### 2.1 Current State

Patient data is:
- Stored in paper files across departments  
- Difficult to access in real time  
- Frequently duplicated or incomplete  

This prevents a **single, accurate patient view**.

---

### 2.2 Impact Analysis

#### 💰 Financial Impact
- £4.2M annual loss due to inefficiencies  
- Staff time wasted retrieving records  
- High cost-per-patient episode  

#### 🏥 Patient Safety Impact
- 18% increase in medication errors  
- Causes:
  - Illegible handwriting  
  - Missing history  
  - No automated alerts  

#### ⚖️ Regulatory Risk
- Risk of non-compliance with CQC  
- Potential penalties and reputational damage  

---

### 2.3 Root Cause Analysis

2016 pilot failed due to:
1. No change management  
2. No structured training  
3. Poor workflow alignment  

> **Root Cause:** Failure in implementation approach, not technology.
